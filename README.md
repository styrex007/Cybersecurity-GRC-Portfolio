# Case Study: IT Security Controls & Compliance Assessment

## Project Overview
This project consists of a comprehensive security controls and compliance audit conducted for **Botium Toys**, a mock business infrastructure used to simulate real-world technical and administrative risk environments. The assessment evaluates the company's current security posture, identifies critical vulnerabilities, and outlines systemic remediation strategies to align corporate operations with international regulatory frameworks.

## Project Deliverables
* 📄 [Final Security & Compliance Report (PDF)](./final_controls_compliance_report.pdf) — Full executive report containing audit checklists and strategic recommendations.

---

## Technical Scenario & Gaps
Botium Toys lacked essential defense-in-depth measures, leaving its data environment highly vulnerable to external threat actors, internal misuse, and massive non-compliance fines. 

### Key Findings:
1. **Network Security:** Absolute lack of perimeter defenses (No Firewall or Intrusion Detection Systems deployed).
2. **Identity & Access Management (IAM):** Failure to enforce the Principle of Least Privilege or Separation of Duties; lack of centralized password management.
3. **Regulatory Violations:** Non-compliance with **PCI DSS** (unencrypted credit card data processing) and **GDPR** (lack of a mandatory 72-hour breach notification incident response plan).

---

## Frameworks & Standards Applied
During this assessment, controls were mapped and audited against the following regulatory compliance domains:
* **PCI DSS:** Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (Financial transaction security)
* **GDPR:** General Data Protection Regulation (EU consumer privacy protection)
* **SOC 2 / SOC 1:** System and Organization Controls (Trust Services Criteria: Security, Confidentiality, and Data Integrity)
